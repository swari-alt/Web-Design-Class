Amager Spine

Urban Design
2025 &Photoshop, InDesign
Community Feedback
Coding Data
Regions
Visual

While studying abroad in Copenhagen, I participated in an urban design studio focused on the redevelopment of an underutilized site. The project began with precedent studies and field research, including semi-structured interviews with residents in the Amagerbro district. Qualitative data from these interviews was systematically coded to identify recurring spatial, social, and programmatic needs. These insights informed a data-driven design strategy, resulting in context-responsive proposals for each quadrant of the site, grounded in a community-centered framework. Alongside my partner, we produced "Amager Spine"

Fordham Gate Reimagined

Spatial Design
2024 & SketchUp, InDesign
Current Context
Planning
Design
Final

My goal was to design urban furniture that highlights the nuances of human interaction with land and its distortion, while preserving culture. My proposal aimed to redefine systems of separation. In response to Asthma Alley and the spatial vulnerabilities of the Bronx, I reimagined the gate as a machine powered by the sounds of the streets—what makes the Bronx unique—that moves along Fordham Street to purify the air, benefiting both Fordham and the surrounding community.

Haus & Home

2024  ·  2:01

I explored the evolution of culture and its representation in the home, blending my Papua New Guinean roots with the American culture I was raised in. Using materials like cardboard, acrylic paint, and hay, I constructed a house that embodies this fusion. Additionally, I developed my visual skills through videography for my final project, Home.
